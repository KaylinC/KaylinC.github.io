The Journey of Art

Through this website, one will be able to attain a basic understanding of the major art periods, some unique characteristics and leading artists throughout history.

Byzantine

Art during the Byzantine period was focused on themes found in the Christian religion. Paintings were very two dimentional with little perspective, making the painting very flat.

Renaissance

The Renaissance was the rebirth of classical art. Artits began turning towards the Greek and Romans mehtods of painting and experiment with new ways of painting such as perspective and light.

Baroque

The Baroque period featured incredibly dramatic scenes that focused on Christian themes. It is unique by its rich color and intense shadowing.

Rococo

In France, the Rococo period emerged as a version of a "late Baroque" period. Art of this period was elaborate, playful emulating King Louis XV's rule.

Neoclassicism

This movement became increasingly popular in response to the declining Rococo period. The subject matter was often darker. People were painted as if they were stone statues with dramatic arm gestures and serious facial experessions.

Romanticism

Paintings of this period were often more dreamy and serene than Neoclassicism. Paintings of this period were often full of emotion that portrayed the awesome power of nature.

Impressionism

Impressionist artists began to use quick, visible brushstrokes to capture a moment in time. These artists began to experiment with light and an emphasis on the effects on it on subject matter.

Cubism

Cubism began to use a composition of shapes and forms in a more abstract manner compared to the traditional naturalistic depictions.

Surrealism

Surrealism grew in popularity after WWI. The art placed realistic objects in unrealistic dreamlike situations that forced the viewer to think more deeply about their sense of reality.

Pop Art

Pop art focused on allowing the general population to understand art and making it more accessible to the average person. The subject matter often would focus on subject matter that would not usually be considered art.
